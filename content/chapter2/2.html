Principles of ETFs valuation
The fair value of an ETF relies on several
characteristics that are public and accessible on
demand from various market data publishers. Four of
the key characteristics are:
1.
Net asset value (NAV)
2.
Intraday Portfolio Value
3.
Cash
4.
Shares outstanding and Creation Units
NAV
The Net Asset Value (NAV) of a fund represents
the total value of all the investments managed by
the fund, which boils down to the assets less the
liabilities.
The NAV is given per ETF share or Creation Unit
(CU) that is outstanding by dividing the total value
of the fund by the number of Creation Units that are
issued and outstanding.
